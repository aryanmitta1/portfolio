01 — About GLASS

What GLASS is.

GLASS — Global Leaders and Scholars in STEM — is an honors program at the NYU Tandon School of Engineering that identifies and supports high-achieving undergraduates with the potential to become global leaders in innovation, technology, research, and entrepreneurship.

The program exists because the complex problems facing humankind — climate, health, security, energy, and communications — now play out on an unprecedented global scale. GLASS prepares engineers to meet those problems not only with technical depth, but with cross-cultural perspective, service, and leadership.

Scholars grow across five core competencies — the "windows" of GLASS — supported by a cohort seminar series, professional coaching, a required semester abroad, and annual funding for research, academics, and professional development. Each scholar connects their work to the Tandon Impact Areas, the UN Sustainable Development Goals, and the NAE Grand Challenges for Engineering.

02 — Mission Statement

Why I engineer.

"To build the avionics, software, and sensing systems that let humanity explore, observe, and protect our world — and to lead engineering teams that solve hard problems with rigor, integrity, and global perspective."

I'm an Electrical & Computer Engineering student drawn to the space between code, circuits, and real-world flight. The systems I care about — rockets, satellites, and autonomous platforms — are some of our most powerful tools for understanding the planet and expanding what's possible beyond it.

Through GLASS, my mission is to pair that technical work with the qualities the program cultivates: academic excellence, professional growth, a commitment to service, global competency, and leadership. I want to be an engineer whose work is technically excellent and directed at challenges that matter to people everywhere.

03 — Global Impact

The goals I'm building toward.

GLASS asks every scholar to anchor their work to global priorities. Here are the ones that map to mine.

UN Sustainable Development Goal

SDG 9 — Industry, Innovation & Infrastructure

My focus on aerospace and avionics sits squarely within SDG 9: building resilient infrastructure and fostering innovation. Earth-observation satellites, telemetry networks, and reliable flight systems are the backbone technologies that let societies monitor, communicate, and respond at a global scale.

  • Resilient infrastructure
  • Technological innovation
  • Earth observation
  • SDG 13 · Climate Action (supporting)
NAE Grand Challenge for Engineering

Engineer the Tools of Scientific Discovery

Avionics, sensor fusion, and embedded systems are the instruments of modern discovery — the spacecraft, telescopes, and autonomous platforms that extend how far and how precisely we can sense the universe. This is the NAE challenge my engineering most directly serves.

  • Tools of scientific discovery
  • Secure cyberspace (embedded)
  • Sensors & instrumentation
