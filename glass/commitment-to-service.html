Commitment
to Service

Engineering is only worth as much as the people it serves. This window is about service learning, mentoring, and community engagement — connecting my technical work to the UN SDGs and the Tandon Impact Areas.

What GLASS means by this

The Commitment to Service window asks scholars to give back — through service learning, community engagement, mentoring, and alternative break experiences. GLASS frames this service around the NYU Tandon Impact Areas and the UN Sustainable Development Goals, so that helping others and advancing global priorities go hand in hand.

My approach

  • Share what I know — teaching and mentoring younger engineers so the next cohort starts further ahead.
  • Make STEM accessible: lower the barrier for students who don't see themselves as "engineering people" yet.
  • Direct service toward the SDGs my work touches — quality education, and resilient infrastructure.
  • Lead by contribution, not just by title — support the teams and events that hold a program together.

Evidence & engagement

  • Teaching Assistant — EG-UY 1004 General Engineering: Communications, Inventory & Purchasing Leader; maintained course materials, managed lab kits, and supported showcase events.
  • NYU Rogue Aerospace (NASA USLI): mentoring teammates on payload electronics and technical design decisions.
  • Peer support and onboarding for new engineering students.
