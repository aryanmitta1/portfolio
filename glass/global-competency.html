Global
Competency

Engineering is a global discipline. Through the GLASS semester abroad and cross-cultural experiences, I'm learning to work across borders — because the challenges I care about don't stop at any one country's edge.

What GLASS means by this

Global Competency is the signature feature of GLASS: a required semester abroad in the fall of sophomore year. Scholars study within NYU's global network — its portal campuses and 30+ partner engineering institutions — gaining the cross-cultural perspective, adaptability, and international experience that define a global engineering leader.

My approach

  • Treat the semester abroad as a chance to study engineering inside a different culture of problem-solving.
  • Build relationships across the GLASS global network that outlast the semester.
  • Bring a global lens back to my aerospace work — space and climate systems are inherently international.
  • Develop the adaptability that comes from learning to operate somewhere unfamiliar.

Evidence & experience

  • GLASS semester abroad within NYU's global network — the program's required cornerstone experience.
  • Engagement with NYU's global engineering partners and portal campuses.
  • A perspective shaped by working on systems — satellites, navigation, climate sensing — that serve a global community.
