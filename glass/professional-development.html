Professional
Development

Turning classroom engineering into a career — through internships, research, industry certifications, and conferences that put my skills to work on real aerospace and embedded-systems problems.

What GLASS means by this

The Professional Development window is about building the experience and network of a working engineer. GLASS supports this with cohort-based career coaching, priority access to select internships and leadership opportunities, the Global Career Accelerator (earning industry certifications), and Agile Apprenticeships that develop real-world project experience.

My approach

  • Seek aerospace and avionics internships where I can ship software and test systems that fly.
  • Pair every role with a concrete technical artifact — tooling, a pipeline, or a validated subsystem.
  • Use GLASS funding and the Global Career Accelerator to add certifications that deepen my embedded and ML skill set.
  • Present and network at engineering conferences to learn from practitioners beyond NYU.

Evidence & experience

  • Blue Origin — Avionics Software Engineering Intern: embedded software and test tooling for flight hardware.
  • Kearfott Corporation — Aerospace / Systems Engineering Intern: ML-based celestial navigation and full sensor-suite integration on a Jetson platform.
  • Soria Analytics — Software Engineering Intern: data pipelines and semantic filtering for a research terminal.
