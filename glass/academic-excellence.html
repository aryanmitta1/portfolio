Mastering the fundamentals of electrical & computer engineering — and pushing past them through research, design, and the honors curriculum that GLASS builds around its scholars.

What GLASS means by this

Academic Excellence is the foundation of GLASS. Scholars pursue demanding coursework, research, senior design, and thesis work, with priority for nomination to NYU's Leadership Honors Course and access to seminars on leadership, professional development, and project management. Annual funding supports research, design, and innovation projects aligned with institutional priorities.

My approach

  • Hold a strong GPA while taking the hardest, most relevant ECE coursework — not the safest path.
  • Connect classes to real systems: signals, control, embedded design, and machine learning all feed my aerospace work.
  • Treat research and design projects as the place where coursework becomes capability.
  • Use GLASS seminars and funding to go deeper than the standard curriculum allows.

Evidence & coursework

  • GPA 3.93 — B.S. Electrical & Computer Engineering, NYU Tandon.
  • Tau Beta Pi — inducted into the national engineering honor society.
  • Core coursework: Embedded Systems Design, Signals & Systems, Feedback Control, Machine Learning, Computer Architecture, PCB Design.
  • Applied ML & CV research during the Kearfott internship (PyTorch, custom layers, Haversine loss).
